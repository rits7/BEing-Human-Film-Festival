About BEing Human

The Being Human Film Festival aims to give high school students a voice through film making, to share ideas of compassion, understanding and tolerance.

Films submitted to the BHFF are between 1 and 5 minutes long and depict themes that explore and celebrate what we share as human beings. Concepts of compassion and understanding are highly regarded by judges and can be a main or secondary theme.

Language can be a barrier to understanding so we encourage film-makers to use little or no dialogue.

All genres are accepted, documentary, drama, comedy, sci-fi, you name it. We want you to enjoy making your film and sharing what you think Being Human is all about.

This year the festival is open to international entries. All films will be independently judged. Communities can run their own school or inter-school competitions using the results from our judges and your film may become eligible to compete in the international final.

Being Human Film Festival supports the 108 lives project, helping disadvantaged communities in Nepal and around the world.

  • Entries Open 1 Feb 2018
  • Entries Close 6 July 2018
  • Local School Finals 20 July
  • International Final 24 August

Judges

If you have film, TV or video production experience, please consider registering to judge films for the Being Human Film Festival. The process will be managed entirely online.

Local Opportunities

Schools, P&Cs or community groups have the opportunity to hold fundraising events to benefit their school community by running film screenings.

If you would like assistance running your own program in your community or have any other questions please contact us.

History

The BEing Human Film Festival (BHFF ) began as an initiative of Canterbury Girls High School Parents & Citizens organisation and was first held in 2017. Four schools participated, Canterbury Girls High School, Canterbury Boys High School. Ashfield Boys High School and Dulwich High.
